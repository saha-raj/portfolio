There are three core areas I consider in my editorial process: how the story is selected and framed, how it's visually laid out, and how data and technology are used.

In practice, these processes are intertwined and iterative.

The Story

  • The seed idea of a visual story usually emerges from a question pertaining to an existing news context. It could be about a missing piece, or an extrapolation into the future, or about gaining understanding of something complex.
  • But not all interesting questions are newsworthy. Some factors to consider in distinguishing them are:
    • Time relevance – immediate vs broad
    • Public interest vs reader curiosity
    • Going beyond peer publications on the same topic
  • Identify experts in the area, to reference their work or to collaborate with.
  • Imagine the data and hypothesize. If I had all the data I wished for, how much or little would that help in understanding the story?
  • Simplify the story components to find the core idea. Build the story around that anchor point - the one idea I would want the reader to take away, if nothing else.
  • Iterate

Design Philosophy

  • Visual hierarchy & Sequencing: Sketch out a storyboard of scenes. Go to the main idea quickly, maybe even to start with.
  • Does the visual need interaction? Minimize friction.
  • Show everything that I want the reader to know.
  • Layout choices should follow function: Consistent and minimal so things don't get in the way while adding context

Data & Technology

  • Can we use data and technology to show something that is not visible or captured through ground reporting
  • Framework: Being thoughtful about building tools that can be used again in a similar context
  • Being mindful about if/what AI could be used for – to show the invisible? To save labor and time? Any such tool must be designed to have full human oversight and the final say.
  • Data availability. In some cases the lack of available data itself presents a journalistic duty to seek it out and build a story around it
  • What are the variables in the data / simulation? How do we simplify them, tying to the core idea?
