The Story

  • The seed idea of a visual story usually emerges from a question pertaining to an already important news context. It could be about a missing piece, or an extrapolation into the future, or about gaining understanding of something complex.
  • But not all interesting questions are newsworthy. Some factors to consider in distinguishing them:
    • Time relevance
    • Public interest vs reader curiosity
    • Marginal gain above peer publications
  • Iterate through the story components to find the core idea. Build the story around that anchor point - the one idea I would want the reader to take away, if nothing else.

Design Philosophy

  • Does the visual need interaction? Minimize friction.
  • Show everything that you want the reader to know.
  • Visual hierarchy & Sequencing: Go to the main idea quickly, maybe to begin with.
  • Layout choices should follow function: Consistent and minimal so things don't get in the way while adding context

Data & Technology

  • Can we use data and technology to show something that is otherwise not visible or captured through ground reporting
  • Being thoughtful about building tools that can be used again in a similar context - framework
  • Being mindful about if/what AI would be used for - to show the invisible? or to save labor+time? Any such tool must be designed to have full human oversight and the final say.
  • Data availability. In some cases the lack of available data itself presents a journalistic duty to seek it out and build a story around it
